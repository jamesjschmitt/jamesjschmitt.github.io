ICE and CPD Detainees Since 2019

Data Visualizations

Here are two visualizations to provide more understanding of the project.

________________________________

   

This line chart shows how the number of detainees held by ICE and CBP has shifted over time. While ICE was the primary agency for detentions in 2019, CBP began holding more detainees during periods of increased migration at the southern border. The chart highlights how enforcement priorities and border policies have influenced which agency is doing most of the detaining.

This ribbon chart breaks down who is being detained, based on criminal history. It shows that the majority of detainees are classified as immigration violators with no criminal record. Individuals with pending charges or convictions make up a smaller portion. The graph underscores how detention often affects those without ties to criminal activity.
